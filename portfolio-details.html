Background

During the COVID pandemic, ANA, a Japanese airline, aimed to offer customers a virtual travel experience. To fulfill this goal, We developed "ANAGranwhale," a metaverse platform allowing players to explore Japanese landmarks in virtual reality. This not only addresses the issue of ANA frequent flyers being unable to utilize their travel miles during real time traveling, but also serves as a platform for discovering places to visit in Japan post-pandemic.

ANA GranWhale Challenges

  • Creating UI elements that matches the app concept.
  • Deciding which UI elements for different information architecture within the game.

ANA GranWhale Solutions

  • Focusing on the usability of the game by refering back to the stats from initial Beta testing.

    Community Feature

    Our initial Beta testing showed statistics of 83% of the users leaving the tour after 10 minutes and 62 % of them leaving empty handed. Through heat map and usability testing, we discover the users wants to socialize prior to purchasing items. We then added community feature with private and open chats that focus on the concept of touring together. This small yet significant feature leads to doubling the number of users and 55% of them stayed in the game for more than 15 minutes.

Background

While developing ANAGranwhale, we encountered a problem item possession. Customers’s travel miles is the main key of wanting to play this game and purchase ANA’s virtual items through JPY. However, similar to traditional gaming, virtual item loses its value as soon as user stop playing the game. As a solution, we created a separate ecommerce site called "ANAGranWhale NFT Marketplace" for digital assets.

NFT Marketplace Challenges

  • User wants to import his or her own customizable items which are not available in ANAGranWhale.
  • Due to ANAGranWhale being a heavy app, users are frustrated with purchasing speed.

NFT Marketplace Solutions

  • Import cryptocurrency with opportunity for users to purchase NFT items that are tied to ANAGranwhale.
  • Changing purchasing flow from in-game purchase to click-to-browser purchase.
  • Adding time limits to user's payment process to increase security during purchasing items.

    Payment methods

    The marketplace’s goal is to create a platform for artists and creators to sell their works as NFTs. However, with only 30.8% awareness and 3.2% ownership of NFTs in Japan, adoption is relatively low. (NFT認知率は3割、保有は3％ スポーツ・ アニメが人気 MMD調査) . To address this, we introduced an additional payment option: users can pay with either cryptocurrency or credit cards. This allows those unfamiliar with cryptocurrency can purchase NFTs easily and securely, similar to regular online shopping. Additionally, since many users took time in choosing payment methods as well as studying how to create wallet for digital currency, a 15-minute payment time limit was added for both methods to enhance security.

    Search Filter

    Heat map revealed that the search filter was the most frequently used feature, as users often explored various categories extensively.
